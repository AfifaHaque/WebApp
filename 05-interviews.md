# Interviews

## Introduction

Although most of the information was collected informally or through a survey, to better understand the challenges students face in managing their academic responsibilities, interviews were conducted with two university students. The objective was to identify common issues related to deadline management, study planning, and the use of existing productivity tools.

## Interview Questions

1. Do you use dedicated study planners?
2. During heavy academic periods, do you lose track of assignment deadlines and exam schedules?
3. Does the usage of multiple applications and their notifications overwhelm you?
4. What are your thoughts on a centralized application to track educational requirements?

## Interview 1

**Participant:** Anika Nawrin Taspi

**Academic Background:** 4th Year EEE Student, BRAC University

### Responses

*Q: Do you use dedicated study planners?*

**A:** No. She does not use a dedicated study planner but uses NotebookLM to organize lecture slides and study materials.

*Q: During heavy academic periods, do you lose track of assignment deadlines and exam schedules?**

**A:** She primarily relies on her phone's notes application and sticky notes to keep track of deadlines and important academic tasks.

*Q: Does the usage of multiple applications and notifications overwhelm you?*

**A:** Yes. She stated that the large number of notifications from different applications can be overwhelming. Important announcements regarding examinations and assignment deadlines may sometimes be missed, causing unnecessary stress. In several cases, she became aware of important information only after hearing it from friends.

*Q: What are your thoughts on a centralized application to track educational requirements?*

**A:** She is open to using such an application, provided that the user interface is simple and user-friendly.

## Interview 2

**Participant:** Tahoshin Tanzim Aoyon

**Academic Background:** 4th Year CSE Student, Independent University, Bangladesh (IUB)

### Responses

*Q: Do you use dedicated study planners?*

**A:** Yes. He currently uses Focus To-Do and Google Calendar to manage academic tasks and schedules.

*Q: During heavy academic periods, do you lose track of assignment deadlines and exam schedules?*

**A:** He occasionally loses track of deadlines, although not to the extent of missing assignments or examinations.

*Q: Does the usage of multiple applications and notifications overwhelm you?*

**A:** While he is able to manage his workload, he expressed interest in having a more centralized solution rather than relying on multiple applications.

*Q: What are your thoughts on a centralized application to track educational requirements?*

**A:** He was highly interested in the concept. He suggested incorporating a time-blocking feature for study scheduling and a file management system that would allow students to organize course materials effectively.

## Key Findings

The interviews revealed that students often rely on multiple tools to manage their academic responsibilities, including note-taking applications, calendars, and productivity tools. This fragmented approach can make it difficult to keep track of deadlines and important announcements.

Both interviewees expressed interest in a centralized platform for academic planning. Important features identified during the interviews include:

* Deadline and examination tracking
* Study scheduling through time blocking
* Centralized organization of academic information
* File management for course materials
* A simple and user-friendly interface

These findings support the development of a Study Planner application that consolidates academic planning and organization into a single platform.
