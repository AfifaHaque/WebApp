# Survey Results

## Survey Objective

The purpose of this survey was to understand how students manage their academic responsibilities, the challenges they face in tracking deadlines, and their interest in a centralized Study Planner application.

## Survey Methodology

The survey was conducted using Google Forms and distributed to university students. Responses were collected anonymously to encourage honest feedback.

## Survey Questions
1. What is your current academic level?
2. How many courses are you taking this semester?
3. Do you currently use any method to plan your studies?
4. Which tools do you use for managing academic tasks?
5. How often do you miss assignments or project deadlines?
6. What is your biggest challenge in managing your academic workload?
7. Would you use a Study Planner application designed specifically for students?
8. Which features would you find most useful?
9. How important are reminders and notifications for academic tasks?

### Study Planning Habits

From Figure 1, we can observe that inspite of having multiple courses, the majority does not use any sort of study planning method. Additionally, from Figure 2, we can observe that most students are either using their notes app to track academic tasks or they are not using any tool at all. Some students however, are using multiple apps dedicated to different uses 

Figure 2 also shows that although the majority of participants rarely or never missed their assignments, time management and maintaining study consistency is a major challenge. 

Figure 3 displays that 43.8% of the participants expressed the desire for a centralized app  and 32.8% of the participants are open to using the app depending on its features


### Desired Features

Figure 3 shows that study schedules and exam reminders are the most desired features with a large importance placed on receiving notifications from the app

## Conclusion

The survey results indicate that students would benefit from a centralized platform that helps them manage academic tasks, schedules, and deadlines more effectively. The findings support the development of the proposed Study Planner system.
